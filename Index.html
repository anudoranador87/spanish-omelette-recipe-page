Spanish paella

Paella is Spain's most famous rice dish,
originally from Valencia. It is cooked in a wide pan
to create the socarrat—a delicious crispy layer at the bottom.
Seasoned with saffron and mixed with seafood or meat,
it is a classic Mediterranean meal perfect for sharing.

Ingredients

  • 400g Spanish bomba rice
  • 1 liter chicken or seafood stock
  • 200g shrimp or prawns
  • 150g squid, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 100g green beans
  • Saffron threads
  • Olive oil, salt, and lemon wedges and paprika

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a paella pan and sauté the seafood; then set aside.
  2. Cook the vegetables and garlic, add grated tomato and paprika.
  3. Pour in the stock with saffron and bring to a boil.
  4. Add the rice evenly and cook for 18-20 minutes without stirring.
  5. Place the seafood back on top and let it rest for 5 minutes.
